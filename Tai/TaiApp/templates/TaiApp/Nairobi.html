This is a one day tour that will be facilitated to those interested in the city tour which is a free tour but charges are applicable where necessary (entry in museums and any other facilities of choice)
0700hrs- Start of the tour.
The tour will start after breakfast from your hotel/area of residence, where our guide will come to pick you up, you shall only drive or use any means of transport depending on your pick up area and the distance to the city. This will be a walking tour hence you are required to have favorable clothes and shoes for the day.

0800hrs- Arrive in the city.
Visit the Memorial Park.
On arrival in the city the first place to visit will be the memorial park, this used to house the former U.S Embassy which was attacked by terrorist in the year 1998. It has a nice picnic area and serene environment for chitchat and relaxing. A brief historic tie between the American government and the Iraq government will be shared and what lead to the attack. (Charges are applicable to access the park if one is willing to go inside)
Visit to Railways Museum
The railways museum is just a stone throw away from the memorial park; the museum harbors rich history from the time before Kenya attained its independence and how it was constructed during the colonial period by the Indian slaves, who came face to face with the “MAN EATERS OF TSAVO” and the British Government here in Kenya. (Charges are applicable)
Visit to Khalsa Centre
After construction of the railway the Indians never went back home and found refuge here in Kenya, they built there temples and social amenities such as schools for themselves, the Khalsa Centre being one of them. It has beautiful architectural designs and good history such as the Dewali Festivals and Haree Krishna celebrations.
Visit to the Indian Town
We shall have a walk into the area where the Indians inhabited and settled with their families and established good businesses that helped the early Kenyan Government after they had acquired Independence from the Colonial Government. This includes the schools and temples that they had built for their children and for themselves and the support they received from the Indian Government.
1300hrs- Lunch Break
This will mark the end of the tour unless you want to continue with the afternoon tour which is optional and done upon request.

NB: The tour is free for those who have booked a safari with us but chargeable to another party that is interested in the tour.
